Introduction

Now that The Summer I Turned Pretty has ended with Belly ending up with Conrad, I want to see if ChatGPT thinks differently and how it analyzes the pros and cons of each relationship Belly has with the Fisher boys.

Hence, this is the prompt I asked ChatGPT: “Do you think Jeremiah or Conrad is better suited for Belly in The Summer I Turned Pretty?”

ChatGPT’s Response

Ah, the classic Belly love triangle debate! Let’s break it down thoughtfully.

Jeremiah:

  • Pros: Jeremiah has been a consistent presence in Belly’s life since childhood. He’s loyal, caring, and emotionally grounded. He knows her deeply, often showing a natural, easy-going kind of love. There’s comfort and stability with him—he’s reliable and attentive to her needs.
  • Cons: Some might argue that his love is more “safe” or predictable. While sweet, he may lack the intensity or dramatic spark that comes with first love or complicated romance.

Conrad:

  • Pros: Conrad represents passion, mystery, and intensity. His relationship with Belly is full of emotional highs and lows, which can be thrilling and transformative. He forces Belly to confront her feelings and grow in ways that Jeremiah doesn’t.
  • Cons: Conrad can be emotionally distant, moody, or inconsistent, which makes the relationship more turbulent. This instability could lead to heartbreak or tension over time.

Overall Fit:

  • If you value stability, loyalty, and deep-rooted friendship, Jeremiah seems better suited.
  • If you value intense emotional connection and transformative experiences, Conrad feels more like the “right” choice for the drama and romance of youth.

Honestly, a lot depends on whether you think the story is about growing into love or finding someone who challenges and ignites you.

If you want, I can also break it down based on how Belly herself grows throughout the series, which might make the “better match” even clearer. Do you want me to do that?

My Response

Honestly, I do not like ChatGPT’s response. It forgot to take into account Jeremiah’s infidelity as well as his commitment issues. Maybe this is ChatGPT’s way of not spoiling the entire TV show to me. On the other hand, ChatGPT seems to understand the personality of the boys better. It accurately matched Conrad as a moody and sometimes emotionally distant person (SPOILER: maybe due to the fact that his mom passed away) and Jeremiah as an easy-going kind of love when compared to his brother.

I decided to follow up with another question for ChatGPT: “Can we analyze which relationship offers stronger personal growth for Belly?”

ChatGPT’s Second Response

Absolutely! Let’s look at Belly’s personal growth through the lens of each relationship.

Belly + Conrad: Growth Through Challenge

  • Emotional Maturity: Conrad is intense and sometimes distant, which forces Belly to navigate complex feelings, including jealousy, insecurity, and unrequited love. This challenges her to understand her own emotions and boundaries more deeply.
  • Self-Awareness: Being with Conrad pushes Belly to reflect on what she truly wants versus what she thinks she should want. She learns to confront uncomfortable truths about love, desire, and disappointment.
  • Resilience: Their relationship isn’t smooth—there’s heartbreak and confusion—which teaches Belly resilience and coping skills she wouldn’t develop in a calmer relationship.
  • Catalyst for Change: Conrad often represents a mirror for Belly. His moods and intensity force her to grow into someone more self-aware and independent, rather than simply going along with the flow.

Summary: The Conrad relationship is a crucible for emotional growth. It’s messy but transformative.

Belly + Jeremiah: Growth Through Stability

  • Trust & Communication: With Jeremiah, Belly learns about emotional honesty and the power of steady, dependable love. She develops the ability to nurture a relationship without drama.
  • Self-Knowledge: While less tumultuous, their bond helps Belly understand her own needs for security, intimacy, and mutual support.
  • Emotional Support: Jeremiah provides a safe space for Belly to explore her identity, which encourages gradual self-confidence and self-esteem.
  • Healthy Boundaries: The relationship models consistency and respect, teaching Belly that love doesn’t always have to be intense to be meaningful.

Summary: The Jeremiah relationship cultivates gradual, sustainable growth and reinforces healthy relational skills.

Comparing the Two

  • Conrad = growth through challenge and self-confrontation. Belly becomes more emotionally complex and resilient but faces a lot of heartbreak along the way.
  • Jeremiah = growth through support and stability. Belly becomes more secure in herself and her capacity to give and receive love, with fewer dramatic lessons.

Insight: If the story’s goal is to show Belly gaining emotional depth and self-discovery under pressure, Conrad drives more intense personal growth. If the focus is on learning about lasting love and healthy emotional habits, Jeremiah fosters that growth more naturally.

If you want, I can make a side-by-side chart comparing how each relationship impacts Belly in emotional, relational, and personal development terms, which makes it really easy to see which pushes her further. Do you want me to do that?

My Second Response

I disagree with its take on Jeremiah but the take on Conrad is pretty accurate. I think Conrad really pushes Belly to reflect and understand herself more in a way that brings out the best in her (if you couldn’t tell, I am #TeamConrad!) by understanding what she wants for herself. Jeremiah, on the other hand, seemed to be a easy, convenient love for Belly (minus the cheating arc). In other words, I saw more growth from Belly when she was with Conrad than when she was with Jeremiah. Additionally, she seemed to love Conrad more throughout the entire series.

Final Takeaways

What does this conversation tell me about ChatGPT? Well, my final thoughts are that ChatGPT’s ability to read characters and people in general is somewhat lacking. It can identify and articulate clear personality traits and sometimes relationship dyanmics as it did with Conrad’s intensity and Jeremiah’s easy-going nature. However, ChatGPT can miss important plot-specific details that significantly affect how a character’s relationship should be analyzed. This shows that while it understands archetypes and patterns, it may avoid spoilers or fail to fully incorporate narrative context.

Overall, ChatGPT is a strong tool for structuring literary analysis and generating insights about relationships and character growth, but it is limited by its tendency to generalize and avoid plot-sensitive details (which does not surprise me for some reaosn).
